Main message from each figure:

1. (Fig. 2) Evidence supporting portfolio effect. The groundfish community as a whole showed greater stability than individual species.
 
(i) Portfolio effect as a buffer against ecosystem perturbation.
(ii) EVOS magnitude vs. magnitude of other perturbations
(iii) EVOS in the context of other perturbations (climate change, etc)

Just sketching out some rough thoughts:
Another type of portfolio effect: 'redundancy' among rare and medium density species (redundancy is probably not exactly the right concept here). Compositional turnover was driven by rare and medium density species; community composition with respect to the most abundant species (probably Arrowtooth) did not show spatial turnover: compositional turnover was not apparent when data were transformed to emphasize the influence of the most abundant species such as Arrowtooth Flounder (i.e. Hellinger transformation; Fig. 6b); instead, turnover was only apparent when data were transformed to allow rare and medium-density species equal chance of explaining community structure (i.e. Wisconsin - Square root transformation; Fig. 6a). In other words, all the spatial change is going on at the level of lower trophic-level species, while top predators show similar spatial composition (we could check if this is supported by those heat maps Ole created last year).
Idea of a diversity cascade, and idea of portfolio effect driven by predation pressure (one of many hypotheses for portfolio effect).
