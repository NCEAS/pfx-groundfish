Results

Diversity and stability

      Knowing the ecological theory behind the diversity-stability relationship, we wanted to examine the mechanisms supporting this relationship in the groundfish community in the GOA.  We began by examining a few diversity metrics within each of the 9 shallow and 5 deep study areas to understand how diversity varied across this large marine ecosystem.  Species richness at each study area was relatively low (across the 31 years), and ranged from approx. 9 - 13 in shallow areas and 11 - 14 in deep areas with the largest difference between any two sites just 4 species (Figure 2 a, b).  Western-most shallow sites (# 8, 9) had higher species richness ADD STATS HERE, and those sites east of Kodiak Island had lower richness.  Alpha diversity (exp H') varied little between study areas, and averaged approx. 9 species across both shallow and deep sites (Figure 2 c, d).  Shallow site 9 again had slightly higher alpha diversity on average, but also had higher variability and ADD STATS HERE, while deep site 13, at the western end of Kodiak Island, had the lowest diversity ADD STATS HERE.  Beta diversity was also remarkably similar across both shallow and deep areas, and averaged approx. 9 in shallow areas and 5 in deep areas (Figure 2 e, f).  Overall, across these 31 years, taxonomic richness and diversity of the groundfish community varied little across the central GOA.

Community composition

     FD depends on who is there, to a large extent, so we examined community composition. 
NMDS indicated spatial turnover in community composition in both shallow and deep study areas along a longitudinal gradient (Fig. 4). For both shallow and deep areas, PERMANOVA indicated that between-area differences were significant (Shallow: p = 0.005, R\(^2\)\(^{ }\) = 0.222; Deep: p = 0.005, R\(^2\)\(^{ }\)= 0.23) and a test of homogeneity of within-area dispersions affirmed that PERMANOVA results were attributable to real between-area differences in community composition (Shallow: p = 0.94, Deep: p = 0.89).
For either results or discussion: For shallow areas, PERMANOVA indicated that although both spatial and temporal processes had significant effects on community composition, between-area variation was greater than temporal variation in community composition (F = 42.8 and 25.4, respectively). For deep areas, between-area variation had similar importance to temporal variation (F = 27.3 and 24.4, respectively).

CVs of the regional metacommunity (shallow sites 0.073; deep sites 0.187) were lower than mean CVs of local communities (shallow sites 0.171 +- 0.043; deep sites 0.253 +- 0.077; Fig. 3).
