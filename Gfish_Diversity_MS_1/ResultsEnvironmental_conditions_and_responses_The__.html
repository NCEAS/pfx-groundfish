Functional diversity:
Rao's Q was similar among most shallow and deep ecoregions (Fig. 3f,g), with the exception of a few shallow regions: a Tukey test indicated significant pairwise differences between shallow regions 6-1, 6-2, 6-4, and 9-2 (p < 0.05). For deep ecoregions, a 1-way ANOVA indicated no significant differences in Rao's Q between ecoregions (p = 0.071).

Community composition

nMDS indicated spatial turnover in community composition in both shallow and deep regions along a longitudinal gradient (Fig. 4). For both shallow and deep ecoregions, PERMANOVA indicated that between-ecoregion differences were significant (Shallow: p = 0.005, R\(^2\)\(^{ }\) = 0.222; Deep: p = 0.005, R\(^2\)\(^{ }\) = 0.23) and a test of homogeneity of within-ecoregion dispersions affirmed that PERMANOVA results were attributable to real between-region differences in community composition (Shallow: p = 0.94, Deep: p = 0.89).
(When only the most dominant species were allowed to contribute to the ordination (ie substituting a Hellinger transformation for a wisconsin-square root transformation), spatial turnover was not apparent among eastern shallow regions (regions 1-7) and diminished among eastern deep regions (regions 1-4). This suggests that, among eastern regions, spatial turnover in community composition was driven by subdominant (but not dominant) species).
